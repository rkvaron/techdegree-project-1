Summary of Experience

Rachel K. Varon most recently was the Director of the Marcus Institute for Digital Education on the Arts (MIDEA) and the Director of Special Projects for the New Media Consortium (NMC), an international consortium of nearly 300 world-class universities, colleges, museums, research centers, and technology companies. She is recognized throughout the state of Texas as an expert in the application of digital media technologies and museum educational environments.

Rachel worked on all museum-related projects for the NMC. She ran a bi-annual minigrant program where awardees complete digital media projects using targeted funds from the Edward and Betty Marcus Foundation. She managed the Steve in Action Project, a social tagging initiative that included collaboration between 10 of the top art museums in the United States. In 2010, Rachel directed the HP Ed Tech Innovator Award program in coordination with Hewlett Packard that funded over $400,000 of tablet PC devices worldwide. Rachel was involved with the creating of over 75 flash-based interactive websites as part of the Digital Education Project for Texas Art Museums.
